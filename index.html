Splatography: Sparse multi-view dynamic Gaussian Splatting for film-making challenges

Anonymous Submission
Supp. Material Code
Extended Abstract:

Deformable Gaussian Splatting (GS) accomplishes photorealistic dynamic 3-D reconstruction from dense multi-view video (MVV) by learning to deform a canonical GS representation. However, in film-making, where budgets are tight, the state-of-the-art (SotA) suffers with sparse MVVs capturing a diverse pool of costumes and practical effects. To address these challenges, we propose a new scene layout that splits the Gaussians and the deformation field into foreground and background components using a sparse set of masks for frames at $t=0$. New loss functions are proposed to enhance canonical pre-training and different parameters are modeled for either deformation field. We suppress background feature expressivity (only modeling point displacement) to ensure that complex view-dependent or dynamic foreground texture effects are not under-reconstructed. Experiments on 3-D and 2.5-D entertainment datasets show that our method produces SotA visual and metric results. Overall, our method produces high quality segmented dynamic reconstruction (including transparent and dynamic textures) with little effort and minimal mask intervention, relying only on sparse MVV observations.
